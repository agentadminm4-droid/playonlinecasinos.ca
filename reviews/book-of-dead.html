Game Basics

Book of Dead is a 5-reel, 10-payline slot with an Egyptian theme. The Book of Dead symbol serves as both wild and scatter — substituting for other symbols and triggering the Free Spins feature when 3+ appear.

Bet range is $0.01 to $100 per spin at most Ontario casinos. The game has a gamble feature that lets you risk winnings on red/black or suit picks.

Free Spins Feature

Three or more Book of Dead scatters trigger 10 free spins. Before the feature starts, one symbol is randomly chosen as a special expanding symbol. During free spins, this symbol expands to fill the entire reel whenever it appears, paying on all 10 paylines regardless of position.

Free spins can be retriggered, and it's possible to retrigger multiple times. Hitting the maximum of 5 reels with Rich Wilde (the highest-paying symbol) as the expanding symbol pays the 5,000x max win.

RTP and Volatility

Book of Dead has a 96.21% RTP — above the industry average. Volatility is high, meaning long dry spells punctuated by big wins. Sessions can be brutal, but the max win is achievable in the free spins feature.

The gamble feature is risky — it can double or quadruple winnings, but the odds are worse than 50/50. Most experienced players turn it off and rely on the base game and free spins.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Book of Dead similar to Book of Ra?
Yes — Book of Dead is widely considered the spiritual successor to Novomatic's Book of Ra. Same theme, similar mechanics, but with modern graphics and a higher RTP (96.21% vs ~92%). Book of Ra is not available in Ontario.
What's the best strategy for Book of Dead?
There's no strategy that changes the RTP. High-volatility slots like this require bankroll discipline: bet 1% or less of your session budget per spin, and accept that you may have many losing spins before a big win.
Can I play Book of Dead for free?
Yes — most Ontario casinos offer a demo mode. Look for the ‘play for fun’ or ‘demo’ button on the game tile. Note that demo play does not count toward real-money wagering requirements on bonuses.
